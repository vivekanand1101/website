01. About

I grew up in a small village called Kerai in Bihar. My father bought our first computer in 2004 and all I did was play Road Rash and EA Cricket on it. In 2008, I went to DPS Panipat City for school, where I represented Panipat in the U-14 and U-16 cricket teams. Leather ball, proper matches, the real deal.

I've been writing software professionally for 8+ years now. Backend systems, databases, payments infrastructure, KYC platforms, real-time systems serving millions of users. I was a Google Summer of Code 2016 student with Fedora, contributing to Pagure and pagure-importer. That was my entry into open source.

I think best in a terminal. My editor is Vim, my shell is zsh. I build with Python, Go, TypeScript, Django, MySQL, and whatever else the problem demands. Lately, I've been deep into agentic engineering, building with Claude and Codex.

I captained my college cricket team at IIIT Allahabad and led them to their first ever tournament win at IIIT Gwalior in 2017. Later at Innovaccer, I picked up table tennis, got surprisingly good at it, and also opened the batting in the office tennis-ball cricket league, winning a couple of trophies. These days I'm back in Kerai, living with my parents, enjoying morning walks and chai while working remotely for Virtual Internships.
